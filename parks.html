What do I need to know about driving off-road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.

Badlands

  • Located in South Dakota.
  • Characterized by rugged canyons, towering spires, and unique eroded buttes.
  • Rich in fossil deposits, providing insights into ancient mammals like the rhinoceros and saber-toothed cat.
  • Offers striking scenic views, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Denali

  • Located in Alaska.
  • Home to the tallest peak in North America, Denali (formerly known as Mount McKinley).
  • Encompasses six million acres of wilderness.
  • Rich biodiversity includes grizzly bears, wolves, caribou, and Dall sheep.

Everglades

  • Situated in Florida, it's the largest tropical wilderness of any kind in the U.S.
  • Known for its unique ecosystem of wetlands, mangroves, and sawgrass marshes.
  • Home to a variety of wildlife, including alligators, manatees, and a diverse array of bird species.
  • Designated as an International Biosphere Reserve and a World Heritage Site.
